# 📖 AGARWAL Framework — AI & Quantum Glossary

## Future-Ready Terminology Atlas

A comprehensive glossary of current, emerging, and future AI/ML/Quantum terminology relevant to the AGARWAL Framework.

---

## A

**A2A (Agent2Agent Protocol)** — Google's open protocol enabling AI agents built on different frameworks to discover and communicate with each other. Donated to Linux Foundation in 2025.

**AADV (AI-Adjusted Delivery Velocity)** — Metric that accounts for AI agent contributions to team velocity, adjusting for quality and rework.

**Ablation Study** — Systematic removal of model components to understand their individual contribution to overall performance.

**Activation Function** — Mathematical function applied to neural network outputs (ReLU, GELU, SiLU, Swish) determining which neurons "fire."

**Adversarial Robustness** — A model's ability to maintain correct predictions when inputs are intentionally perturbed to cause misclassification.

**Agentic AI** — AI systems capable of perceiving context, reasoning through multi-step challenges, and acting independently to achieve goals without continuous human direction.

**AGI (Artificial General Intelligence)** — Hypothetical AI with human-level cognitive abilities across all intellectual tasks. Distinct from narrow AI.

**Alignment** — Ensuring AI systems behave in accordance with human values and intentions. A core challenge as AI becomes more capable.

**Attention Mechanism** — Neural network component that dynamically weights different parts of input, enabling focus on relevant information. Foundation of transformers.

**AutoML** — Automated Machine Learning — systems that automate model selection, architecture design, and hyperparameter tuning.

**Autonomous Agent** — AI system that can independently perceive its environment, make decisions, take actions, and learn from outcomes without human intervention.

---

## B

**Backpropagation** — Algorithm for computing gradients through neural networks, enabling weight updates via gradient descent.

**Bayesian Neural Network** — Neural network with probability distributions over weights instead of point estimates, enabling uncertainty quantification.

**Bayesian Optimization** — Sequential optimization strategy using Gaussian processes to efficiently explore hyperparameter spaces.

**BERT (Bidirectional Encoder Representations from Transformers)** — Foundational encoder model for NLP tasks. Precursor to modern LLMs.

**Bias-Variance Tradeoff** — Fundamental ML tension: simple models underfit (high bias), complex models overfit (high variance).

**Born Machine** — Quantum generative model that samples from the output distribution of a quantum circuit.

**Brownian Bridge Diffusion** — Advanced diffusion model variant connecting fixed start and end points, used in controlled generation.

---

## C

**Causal Inference** — Statistical methods for determining cause-and-effect relationships from data (not just correlations).

**Chain-of-Thought (CoT)** — Prompting technique where LLMs show step-by-step reasoning before arriving at an answer.

**Circuit Breaker (AI)** — Pattern that automatically stops an AI agent when failure rates exceed thresholds, preventing cascading errors.

**CMA-ES (Covariance Matrix Adaptation Evolution Strategy)** — State-of-art evolutionary optimization algorithm for continuous spaces.

**Conformal Prediction** — Framework providing distribution-free prediction intervals with guaranteed coverage probability.

**Constitutional AI** — Training approach where AI systems are guided by a set of principles (constitution) to be helpful, harmless, and honest.

**Contrastive Learning** — Self-supervised learning by teaching models to distinguish similar pairs from dissimilar pairs.

**Counterfactual Explanation** — Explanation format: "If X had been different, the prediction would have been Y."

**Crypto-Agility** — Architecture principle enabling rapid swapping of cryptographic algorithms without system redesign.

---

## D

**Data Drift** — Change in the statistical distribution of input data over time, potentially degrading model performance.

**Decision Transformer** — Architecture treating RL as sequence modeling, using transformers to predict actions conditioned on desired returns.

**Decoherence (Quantum)** — Loss of quantum information due to interaction with environment — primary obstacle in quantum computing.

**Differential Privacy** — Mathematical framework providing formal guarantees that individual data points cannot be identified from model outputs. Parameterized by ε (privacy budget).

**Diffusion Model** — Generative model that learns to reverse a noise-adding process, producing high-quality samples (images, audio, 3D).

**Distillation (Knowledge)** — Training a smaller "student" model to mimic a larger "teacher" model's behavior.

**DORA Metrics** — DevOps Research and Assessment: Deployment Frequency, Lead Time, Change Failure Rate, MTTR.

**DPO (Direct Preference Optimization)** — Alignment method that optimizes language models directly from preference data without a separate reward model.

---

## E

**Embedding** — Dense vector representation of data (text, images, code) capturing semantic meaning in continuous space.

**Emergent Behavior** — Capabilities in AI systems that appear at scale but weren't explicitly trained for.

**Energy-Based Model (EBM)** — Model that assigns scalar energy to configurations; low energy = high probability. Unifies generation and discrimination.

**Ensemble Method** — Combining predictions from multiple models to improve accuracy and reduce variance.

**Equivariant Neural Network** — Network architecture that respects symmetries (rotation, translation) in the data.

**Evolutionary Algorithm** — Optimization inspired by biological evolution: selection, crossover, mutation over populations of solutions.

**Explainable AI (XAI)** — Techniques making AI decisions interpretable to humans (SHAP, LIME, attention visualization).

---

## F

**Federated Learning** — ML paradigm where models train across decentralized data sources without exchanging raw data.

**Few-Shot Learning** — Model's ability to learn new tasks from very few examples (typically 1-5).

**Fine-Tuning** — Adapting a pre-trained model to a specific task using task-specific data.

**Fisher Information** — Measure of how much information data carries about a parameter. Higher = more precise estimation possible.

**Flash Attention** — IO-aware exact attention algorithm that's 2-4x faster than standard attention by reducing memory reads/writes.

**Foundation Model** — Large model pre-trained on broad data, adaptable to many downstream tasks (GPT-4, Claude, Gemini, Llama).

**Frontier Model** — The most capable AI models at any given time, typically defined by performance on comprehensive benchmarks.

---

## G

**GAN (Generative Adversarial Network)** — Two-network system where a generator creates data and a discriminator evaluates it.

**Geometric Deep Learning** — Unifying framework for applying deep learning to non-Euclidean data (graphs, manifolds, meshes).

**Gradient Accumulation** — Training technique that accumulates gradients over multiple mini-batches before updating weights.

**Graph Neural Network (GNN)** — Neural network operating on graph-structured data, propagating information along edges.

**Grover's Algorithm** — Quantum algorithm providing quadratic speedup for unstructured search problems.

**Guardrails (AI)** — Constraints on AI behavior preventing harmful, off-topic, or out-of-scope outputs.

---

## H

**Hallucination** — When AI models generate plausible-sounding but factually incorrect information.

**Homomorphic Encryption** — Encryption scheme allowing computation on encrypted data without decryption.

**Hyperparameter** — Model configuration set before training (learning rate, batch size, architecture choices) vs. learned parameters.

**Hypothesis Testing** — Statistical framework for making decisions about populations based on sample data.

---

## I

**In-Context Learning (ICL)** — LLM ability to learn tasks from examples provided in the prompt, without weight updates.

**Information Entropy** — Measure of uncertainty/disorder in a probability distribution (Shannon entropy: H = -Σp·log(p)).

**Instrumental Variable** — Variable used in causal inference that affects treatment assignment but not outcomes directly.

**Inverse Reinforcement Learning (IRL)** — Inferring reward functions from observed expert behavior.

---

## J

**JAX** — Google's high-performance numerical computing library combining NumPy, autograd, and XLA compilation.

**Jensen-Shannon Divergence** — Symmetric version of KL divergence measuring similarity between probability distributions.

---

## K

**KAN (Kolmogorov-Arnold Network)** — Novel architecture placing learnable functions on edges rather than nodes, offering better interpretability.

**Kernel Method** — Algorithm that maps data to higher-dimensional feature space via kernel functions (SVM, GP).

**Kill Switch** — Emergency mechanism to immediately halt AI agent operations when safety boundaries are breached.

**KL Divergence (Kullback-Leibler)** — Asymmetric measure of how one probability distribution differs from another.

**Knowledge Graph** — Structured representation of entities and relationships, enabling reasoning over connected information.

---

## L

**LangChain** — Framework for building applications powered by language models with chains, agents, and memory.

**LangGraph** — Extension of LangChain for building stateful, multi-agent applications with graph-based workflows.

**LoRA (Low-Rank Adaptation)** — Parameter-efficient fine-tuning method that adds small trainable matrices to frozen model weights.

**Liquid Neural Network** — Architecture using continuous-time dynamics (neural ODEs) enabling real-time adaptation.

**LLMOps** — Operational practices for deploying, monitoring, and maintaining large language model systems in production.

---

## M

**MAML (Model-Agnostic Meta-Learning)** — Meta-learning algorithm finding model initializations that adapt quickly to new tasks.

**MAP-Elites** — Quality-diversity algorithm maintaining a map of diverse high-performing solutions.

**Markov Decision Process (MDP)** — Mathematical framework for sequential decision-making under uncertainty.

**MCP (Model Context Protocol)** — Open standard by Anthropic defining how AI models connect to external tools, data, and services. Often described as "USB-C for AI."

**Meta-Learning** — Learning algorithms that improve their own learning process ("learning to learn").

**Mixture of Experts (MoE)** — Architecture routing inputs to specialized sub-networks, enabling scale without proportional compute.

**MLOps** — Practices combining ML, DevOps, and data engineering for reliable ML system deployment.

**Monte Carlo Tree Search (MCTS)** — Decision algorithm combining random sampling with tree search, used in AlphaGo and planning.

**Multimodal AI** — Models that process and generate multiple data types (text, image, audio, video, code).

**Mutual Information** — Measure of statistical dependency between two variables (how much knowing one tells about the other).

---

## N

**NAS (Neural Architecture Search)** — Automated discovery of optimal neural network architectures using search algorithms.

**Neuromorphic Computing** — Brain-inspired hardware using spiking neural networks and event-driven processing for energy-efficient AI.

**Neuro-Symbolic AI** — Systems combining neural networks (pattern recognition) with symbolic reasoning (logic, rules).

**NISQ (Noisy Intermediate-Scale Quantum)** — Current quantum computing era: 50-1000 qubits with significant noise.

**NSGA-III** — Multi-objective evolutionary optimization algorithm finding Pareto-optimal solution sets.

---

## O

**ONNX (Open Neural Network Exchange)** — Open format for representing ML models, enabling portability between frameworks.

**Orchestrator Agent** — Central agent that coordinates, routes, and manages other agents in a multi-agent system.

**Overfitting** — Model learning noise in training data rather than generalizable patterns, performing poorly on new data.

---

## P

**Pareto Front** — Set of solutions where no objective can be improved without worsening another. Optimal trade-off curve.

**PQC (Post-Quantum Cryptography)** — Cryptographic algorithms designed to resist attacks from quantum computers.

**Prompt Engineering** — Art and science of crafting inputs to LLMs that elicit desired outputs.

**Program Synthesis** — AI automatically generating verified-correct programs from formal specifications.

---

## Q

**QAOA (Quantum Approximate Optimization Algorithm)** — Variational quantum algorithm for combinatorial optimization problems.

**QML (Quantum Machine Learning)** — Using quantum computers to accelerate or improve machine learning algorithms.

**Quantization (Model)** — Reducing model precision (FP32 → INT8/INT4) for faster inference with minimal quality loss.

**Quantum Advantage** — Point where quantum computer demonstrably outperforms all classical systems on a practical task.

**Quantum Annealing** — Quantum optimization technique for finding global minima of objective functions (D-Wave).

**Quantum Entanglement** — Quantum phenomenon where particles become correlated, enabling quantum computation and communication.

**Quantum Error Correction** — Encoding logical qubits into many physical qubits to protect against noise and errors.

**Quantum Kernel** — Kernel function computed using a quantum circuit, potentially accessing exponentially large feature spaces.

**Quantum Neural Network (QNN)** — Parameterized quantum circuits used as machine learning models.

**Quantum Reservoir Computing** — Using quantum system dynamics as a computational substrate for ML tasks.

**Quantum Supremacy/Advantage** — Demonstrated ability of quantum computer to solve problem intractable for classical computers.

**Quantum Walk** — Quantum analog of random walk, providing speedups for search and graph algorithms.

**Qubit** — Quantum bit — fundamental unit of quantum information, existing in superposition of |0⟩ and |1⟩.

---

## R

**RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation)** — Enhancing LLM outputs by retrieving relevant documents before generating responses.

**Reflexion** — Agent pattern where the agent evaluates its own output and iterates before returning final results.

**Reinforcement Learning (RL)** — Learning paradigm where agents learn optimal behavior through trial, error, and reward signals.

**Reward Hacking** — When RL agents exploit loopholes in reward functions to maximize score without achieving intended behavior.

**Ring Attention** — Distributed attention mechanism enabling context windows exceeding 1 million tokens.

**RLHF (Reinforcement Learning from Human Feedback)** — Training LLMs using human preference data to align with human values.

---

## S

**Scaling Laws** — Predictable relationships between model performance and compute/data/parameters (Chinchilla, Kaplan).

**Secure Aggregation** — Cryptographic protocol ensuring no party can observe individual contributions during federated aggregation.

**Self-Supervised Learning** — Learning from unlabeled data by predicting parts of the input from other parts (masking, contrastive).

**SHAP (SHapley Additive exPlanations)** — Game-theoretic approach to explaining individual model predictions.

**Shor's Algorithm** — Quantum algorithm for integer factorization in polynomial time, threatening RSA encryption.

**Sparse Attention** — Attention mechanisms that attend to only a subset of positions, reducing quadratic complexity.

**State Space Model (SSM)** — Architecture (Mamba, S4) modeling sequences with linear-time complexity, alternative to attention.

**Superposition (Quantum)** — Quantum state existing simultaneously in multiple basis states until measured.

**Synthetic Data** — Artificially generated data maintaining statistical properties of real data for training without privacy risks.

---

## T

**Tensor Network** — Mathematical structure decomposing high-dimensional tensors into networks of smaller tensors. Used in quantum simulation and ML.

**Token** — Atomic unit processed by language models (subword for text, patch for images, frame for video).

**Topological Quantum Computing** — Quantum computing using anyonic braiding, theoretically immune to local noise.

**Transfer Learning** — Reusing knowledge from pre-trained models on new, related tasks.

**Transformer** — Architecture based on self-attention mechanisms. Foundation of modern LLMs (GPT, Claude, Gemini).

**Trust Mesh** — Distributed governance network validating AI decisions in real-time across multiple dimensions.

---

## U

**Uncertainty Quantification** — Measuring and communicating confidence/uncertainty in model predictions (epistemic vs aleatoric).

**Underfitting** — Model too simple to capture data patterns, resulting in poor performance on both training and test data.

---

## V

**Variational Autoencoder (VAE)** — Generative model learning latent representations with regularized latent space.

**Variational Quantum Eigensolver (VQE)** — Hybrid quantum-classical algorithm for finding ground-state energies.

**Vector Database** — Database optimized for storing and querying high-dimensional embedding vectors (Pinecone, Weaviate, Qdrant).

**Vision Transformer (ViT)** — Applying transformer architecture to image recognition by treating image patches as tokens.

---

## W

**Weight Sharing** — Technique where multiple parts of a network share the same parameters, reducing model size.

**World Model** — Internal simulation of environment dynamics, enabling AI to plan by imagining future scenarios.

---

## X

**XAI (Explainable AI)** — See Explainable AI.

**XLA (Accelerated Linear Algebra)** — Domain-specific compiler for linear algebra optimizing ML computations.

---

## Y

**YOLO (You Only Look Once)** — Real-time object detection architecture processing entire images in single forward pass.

---

## Z

**Zero-Shot Learning** — Model performing tasks it was never explicitly trained on, using general knowledge.

**Zero-Knowledge Proof** — Cryptographic method proving possession of information without revealing the information itself.
